  • Campus Philly

    Campus Philly is a nonprofit that creates economic growth by encouraging college students to study, explore, live and work in Philadelphia. They had a web based internship platform approaching 10 years old and wanted to meet students where they were in 2016: phones, tablets, and laptops.

    Together we improved Campus Philly's understanding of their audience's behavior as well as activity patterns by creating robust reporting tools.

    For the platform redesign, I led visual and interaction design with the help of James Harrison on engineering and Olivia Haas on user experience.

    • https://careers.campusphilly.org

  • LATCH

    LATCH is a product born out of a user experience study in collaboration with the Appalachian Trail Conservancy where we tried to understand the different audiences of the Trail. Through that we heard a desire for young hikers to share trail experiences with their loved ones back home.

    LATCH is a low-power, ambient journaling app that encourages minimal hands-on useage while recording a hiker's journey metrics and compiling them to a shareable experience tapestry. Above all, LATCH is designed to maximize the in-person experience of being in nature while creating an artifact to keep forever.

    • Kickstarter Launching August 2016

  • Trenton250

    Trenton250 is the framework for the city of Trenton's Mater Plan, culminating on its 250th anniversary. This is a chance to reach the residents of Trenton where they live and give them a hopeful and empowering understanding of the city's plan for improvment.

    Master Plans are usually dusty tomes on forgotten shelves but T250 is an interactive and living document, designed to keep residents in the know as the city makes progress. Larger principles are broken down into smaller strategies and then to atomic actions designed to give the reader full comprehension of the city's intent.

    • Launching Fall 2016
